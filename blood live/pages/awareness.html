Myths vs Facts

Myth: Donating blood hurts a lot.
Fact: You may feel a slight prick of the needle, but the process is largely painless. The satisfaction of saving a life outweighs the momentary discomfort.
Myth: I will get weak after donating blood.
Fact: A healthy adult recovers quickly. The fluid volume is replaced within 24 hours, and red blood cells are regenerated within a few weeks. You can resume normal activities almost immediately.
Myth: I can contract diseases by donating.
Fact: No. Sterile, disposable needles are used for every donor. There is zero risk of contracting HIV or other infections from the donation process itself.
Myth: Vegetarians cannot donate blood.
Fact: Vegetarians can donate blood as long as their iron levels are sufficient. A balanced diet ensures you are eligible.
